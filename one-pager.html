OTel, AI & Observability: What Matters Now

Matthew Reider · April 2026

Distilled from the full brief.

The Market

Observability is roughly a $12B market growing at ~20% annually. Datadog leads at $3.4B revenue (+28% YoY), followed by Dynatrace at $1.7B (+19%) and Grafana Labs at $400M+ ARR (~60% growth). Consolidation is accelerating: Cisco bought Splunk for $28B, New Relic went private at $6.5B, and Snowflake is acquiring Observe for ~$1B.

OpenTelemetry Is Winning

OTel is the #2 CNCF project behind Kubernetes. 48% of enterprises are using it today, with another 50% planning to or evaluating. Traces, metrics, and logs are all GA. Profiling just hit alpha. All three major clouds now support OTLP ingestion natively.

OTel commoditizes telemetry collection and makes switching backends a config change instead of a re-instrumentation project. Vendor differentiation shifts to analytics, AI, UX, and platform breadth.

What This Means for Datadog

Datadog’s proprietary agent is both its moat and its vulnerability. It drives cross-sell into 20+ products – but when customers adopt the OTel Collector instead, Datadog loses control of the data pipeline. The response: DDOT (Datadog Distribution of the OTel Collector), bundling DD-specific processors into a vendor-branded Collector.

Attribute flattening
OTel’s hierarchical model (resource / scope / span) must be collapsed into DD’s flat key:value tags – lossy by design.
Metrics temporality
OTel SDKs default to cumulative; DD requires delta, forcing customer-side conversion.
Query fragmentation
Different DSLs per signal (metrics DSL, Lucene for logs/traces, DDSQL) vs. Dynatrace’s single DQL or Grafana’s OTel-native TraceQL.
AI Observability Is the Growth Story

The AI observability sub-market is projected at $6.8B by 2029 (36% CAGR) – 3x the growth rate of traditional observability. Two dimensions:

AI for observability
Datadog’s Bits AI (LLM-driven correlation) vs. Dynatrace’s Davis AI (causal, topology-aware). Every vendor now ships AI-assisted features.
Observability for AI
Monitoring LLM inference, token costs, agent traces, GPU utilization. DD landed an 8-figure deal with a leading AI model company in Q4 2025.

OTel’s GenAI semantic conventions (model spans, token usage, agent traces) are positioning it as the neutral instrumentation layer for AI workloads – the same role it played for microservices.

Three Takeaways

1. OTel is table stakes. The question isn’t whether to support it – it’s how natively your backend stores and queries OTel data without lossy translation.

2. AI workloads are the next platform shift for observability, and OTel’s GenAI conventions could standardize this market the same way OTLP standardized APM.

3. The competitive moat is moving upstream – from “who collects the data” to “who makes the data actionable” through AI-driven analysis, automation, and developer experience.
